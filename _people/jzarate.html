About me

I am with the Advanced Interactive Technologies lab (AIT) at ETH Zurich. My research focuses on how we interact through the sense of touch, and how we can improve the systems that receive our tactile information and provide us with feedback. Particularly, I am interested in the design and fabrication of systems that enable novel types of interactions.

I completed my M.Sc. and Ph.D. in physics at Instituto Balseiro, Bariloche, Argentina. During my doctoral project I worked on microfabricated infrared sensors at the BT-CAB Laboratory. In 2014, I joined the Soft Transducers Lab (LMTS) at EPFL, Switzerland, to work on haptic displays based on miniaturized electromagnetic actuators. In 2018 I moved to the AIT Lab to work in haptics and wearables.
